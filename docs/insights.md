Sample insights you can talk about in interviews after running the project.

- **Total Revenue:** ~$2.3M across 4 years (2015–2018)
- **Total Profit:** ~$286K
- **Profit Margin:** ~12.5%
- **Total Orders:** ~9,994
- **Total Customers:** ~793 unique
- **Avg Order Value:** ~$230

---

## Top 5 Findings

### 1. The West region is the strongest contributor (~32% of revenue)
The West region consistently outperforms all others in both revenue and profit.
California alone drives a large share of West-region sales.

**Business action:** Replicate West-region marketing playbooks in underperforming regions.

### 2. Technology category is the most profitable despite lower volume
- Technology = ~$836K revenue, ~$145K profit (~17% margin)
- Office Supplies = ~$706K revenue, ~$122K profit (~17% margin)
- Furniture = ~$742K revenue, ~$18K profit (~2.5% margin)

**Business action:** Furniture's margin is dangerously low due to high discount + shipping costs. Renegotiate supplier contracts or reprice.

### 3. Discounts above 40% destroy profitability
Orders with 40–60% discounts run at a negative profit margin. The 21–40% range also has compressed margins.

**Business action:** Cap standard discount at 20%; require approval above that.

### 4. Top 10 customers = ~10% of total revenue
A small number of corporate customers contribute disproportionately to revenue.

**Business action:** Launch a VIP loyalty program for the top 5–10% spenders.

### 5. Q4 is the strongest quarter every year
November–December sales spike by 25–35% compared to monthly average.

**Business action:** Increase inventory and marketing spend in Q3 to capture Q4 demand.
